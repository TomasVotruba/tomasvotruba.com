Why I love reading books? They trigger in-depth, longitudinal inner dialogue focused on a single topic. Reading 1-2 hours in a row helps me think deeply about a topic or a story, build robust neural network and interconnect fresh knowledge with long term memory. As a result, it makes learning new skills more effortless, and keeps my brain more effective.

When I look at this list, I've noticed interesting edge-case similarity. All my favorites book from 2025 were older them be, some of them twise as much. Is it me looking to the past trying to extrapolate timeless experience to integrate in my life? Maybe.

<br>

Without further ado, in random order:


## 1. Coming Out of the Ice: An Unexpected Life - by Victor Herman

<img src="https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/61qy2bYkMZL._SL1017_.jpg" style="float: left; max-width: 10em" class="me-3 mb-3 img-thumbnail">

If you think you're suffering, life is unfair, you're disadvantaged compared to other or just have bad luck, wait till you hear about Victor Herman. Imagine story of Arnold Schwazenegger, just backẃards. From top of the world, USA, his family moved to Russia, near Siberia. What supposed to be 3 years trip, turned into 18 years in prison. For murder? No, for being honest and trying to be helpful.

I've listened to this book while doing heavy lifting house reconstructing, digging dirt at 10 PM at 5 °C till 1 AM. It helped to change my perspective on personal suffering, turning "I feel weak" to "burning midnight oil" as thats the only way. I was shocked to hear about Victor just now, what an amazing story and character.

<br>


## 2. Surely you're Joking, Mr. Feynman - by Mr. Feynman

<img src="https://media.libris.to/jacket/16417596_surely-youre-joking-mr-feynman.jpg" style="float: left; max-width: 10em" class="me-3 mb-3 img-thumbnail">

You may know Mr. Feynman from Youtube philosophy/physics videos with short vitty entertaining answers. So did I, but I wanted to know more about this guy who was one of main people in Manhattan project. But also, he spoke Portuguese, Spanish and Japaneese. He also played drums and died in very boring way, in his own words.

Quite inspiring piece in value knowledge over money, how to pick up girls, how to fell in love with them, how to create a code that nobody except your wife understands, and how to be honest and give feedback even if it costs you a Nobel prize. If you're a knowledge worked, this book is about making love to knowledge through constants experiments. Loved it!

<br>


## 3. 48 Laws of Power - by Robert Greene

<img src="/assets/images/posts/2026/book-power.webp" style="float: left; max-width: 10em" class="me-3 mb-3 img-thumbnail">

The title of this books sounds as a basic marketing catch, and made me ignore this book for quite some time. I finally picked i up and thought to give it a go. If it will be crap in first 3 laws, I'll drop it. It wasn't and I was hooked. Robert connects historical stories of Ancient Greek, Napoleon, Machiavelli, Genghis Khan and WW 2 leaders with modern day leaders in both business and policts. Some methods seems rather harsh, but choosing own family over others gives it different perspective.

I've also read *Mastery*, and just reading *The 33 Strategies of War* from same author. Highly recommended if you want to understand power games in human relationships, be it business or politics.

<br>

## 4. The Power of Ambition - by Jim Rohn

<img src="https://media.libris.to/jacket/37167498_the-power-of-ambition-awakening-the-powerful-force-within-you.jpg
" style="float: left; max-width: 10em" class="me-3 mb-3 img-thumbnail">

Do you know Dale Carnegey? Jim Rohn could be his half-brother. I usually find motivational speakers fake, as they only repeat what you want to hear, but Jim Rohn is a different breed. I find him very honest, open-minded, focused and real. No wonder, he started in 1960s, when this was still a bleeding-edge innovation topic.

I was listening to Jim Rohn when I started to exercise. I found weekly meeting with ever-increasing weight quite a challange. First week, my body hurt 5 days in a rok, 2nd week 4 days in a row, 3rd week again 4 days in a row. I felt like giving up, so this book really helped me to turn fear of sufferting into persistence game. Great book with many jokes in his own account. Very handy if you have a great mind shift as your next goal.

* "But Jim, I'm tired"... "Do it tired".
* "But Jim, I might, fail"... "Do it anyway".

From ["Just do it" video](https://youtu.be/vndqE7jW_jY?t=1093).

<br>

## 5. 36 Views of Mount Fuji - by Cathy Davidson

<img src="/assets/images/posts/2026/book-fuji.jpg" style="float: left; max-width: 10em" class="me-3 mb-3 img-thumbnail">


There is a dozen books on *Ikigai* and *Kaizen*, most of them living of commercial success of these keywords. Saying that, it's hard to find a good, honest and natural book about these topic. I was lucky to find this nearly 30 years old gem.

Published in 1993, Cathy describes her travels from USA to Japan to teach on all-female university. She describes Japanese culture year by year, her love to the culture, ceremonies and duality. For someone like me who travelled to Japan last year, it was a great chance to refresh my memories and compare them with author's perspective from 1980s.

I prefer to experience travelling first hand, but this time and culture snapshot from era I was not even alive was a deep emotinal experience for me.
